Paella

From humble Valencian roots to Spanish national dish

Historical Journey

Paella originated in the mid-19th century near Valencia, Spain, where farm laborers cooked rice in large flat pans over open fires in the fields. The dish was made with local ingredients: rice from the coastal wetlands, vegetables from nearby gardens, rabbits, and snails. The iconic yellow color came later with the introduction of saffron to the recipe.

Ingredient Origins

  • Rice - Introduced by the Moors in the 8th century
  • Saffron - Cultivated in La Mancha region since Arab rule
  • Olive Oil - Ancient Mediterranean staple
  • Seafood - From the Mediterranean coast
  • Paprika - New World spice, integrated after Columbus
